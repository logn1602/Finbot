# Insurance Essentials: Protecting What You Have Built

## The Purpose of Insurance

Insurance transfers financial risk from you to an insurance company in exchange for regular premium payments. You pay a known, manageable cost (the premium) to avoid an unknown, potentially devastating cost (a major loss). Insurance is not an investment — it is a safety net.

## The Core Principle: Insure Against Catastrophe, Not Inconvenience

A good insurance strategy covers events that would be financially ruinous, not events you can absorb from savings. This means:

- **Do insure**: Major medical expenses, disability, liability lawsuits, house fires, car accidents, premature death if others depend on your income.
- **Skip or minimize**: Extended warranties on electronics, flight insurance, insurance on low-value items, pet insurance for routine care.

Raise your deductibles as high as your emergency fund can handle. Higher deductibles lower premiums, and you self-insure the small stuff.

## Health Insurance

Health insurance is typically the most important coverage because medical costs can be astronomical.

**Key concepts:**
- **Premium**: The monthly payment to maintain coverage.
- **Deductible**: The amount you pay out of pocket before insurance kicks in.
- **Copay/Coinsurance**: Your share of costs after meeting the deductible.
- **Out-of-pocket maximum**: The most you will pay in a year; after this, insurance covers 100 %.

**Choosing a plan:**
- **High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) + HSA**: Lower premiums, higher deductible. Best for generally healthy individuals who want the tax benefits of an HSA. The HSA can become a powerful retirement savings tool.
- **Traditional PPO/HMO**: Higher premiums, lower out-of-pocket costs. Better for those with frequent medical needs, chronic conditions, or families with young children.

## Life Insurance

Life insurance replaces your income if you die. It is essential if anyone depends on your earnings — spouse, children, aging parents.

**Term life insurance**: Covers a specific period (10, 20, or 30 years). Pure protection with no investment component. Extremely affordable for healthy individuals. This is the right choice for the vast majority of people.

**Whole/universal life insurance**: Covers your entire life and includes a cash value component. Premiums are 5-15x higher than term. The investment returns are typically mediocre. For most people, buying term insurance and investing the premium difference yields far better results.

**How much coverage?** A common guideline is 10-12 times your annual income, but calculate based on:
- Years of income replacement needed
- Outstanding debts (mortgage, student loans)
- Future expenses (children's education)
- Existing savings and other income sources
- Subtract what your family already has (savings, spouse's income, Social Security survivor benefits)

## Disability Insurance

Your ability to earn income is your most valuable financial asset. Disability insurance replaces a portion of your income (typically 60-70 %) if illness or injury prevents you from working.

- **Short-term disability**: Covers the first 3-6 months. Often provided by employers.
- **Long-term disability**: Kicks in after short-term ends, can last until retirement age. This is the critical coverage.

**Key features to look for:**
- **Own-occupation definition**: Pays if you cannot perform your specific job, not just any job.
- **Non-cancelable and guaranteed renewable**: The insurer cannot raise your premiums or cancel your policy as long as you pay.
- **Inflation rider**: Benefits increase over time to keep pace with inflation.

## Auto Insurance

Mandatory in most places, but coverage levels matter:

- **Liability**: Covers damage you cause to others. Carry at least $100,000/$300,000 or more if you have significant assets to protect.
- **Collision**: Covers damage to your car in an accident. Consider dropping on older vehicles where the premium approaches the car's value.
- **Comprehensive**: Covers theft, weather damage, animal collisions. Same cost-benefit analysis as collision.
- **Uninsured/underinsured motorist**: Covers you if the at-fault driver lacks adequate insurance. Essential.

**Ways to save:** Bundle policies, maintain a clean driving record, take defensive driving courses, raise deductibles, shop around every 2-3 years.

## Homeowners / Renters Insurance

**Homeowners insurance** covers your dwelling, belongings, and liability. Ensure you have replacement cost coverage (not actual cash value, which deducts depreciation). Update coverage when you renovate or make major purchases.

**Renters insurance** is inexpensive ($15-30/month) and covers your belongings and liability. It is one of the most underutilized protections — many renters incorrectly assume their landlord's insurance covers tenant belongings.

**Document your possessions**: Take a video walk-through of your home and store it in the cloud. This makes claims dramatically easier if you ever need to file one.

## Umbrella Insurance

An umbrella policy provides additional liability coverage beyond your auto and homeowners limits. It is inexpensive (often $200-300/year for $1 million in coverage) and essential for anyone with significant assets or high earning potential. It protects against lawsuits, large accident claims, and even some situations not covered by underlying policies.

## Insurance You Probably Do Not Need

- **Credit card payment protection insurance**: Overpriced. Build an emergency fund instead.
- **Mortgage life insurance**: Declining benefit at level premium. A term life policy is cheaper and more flexible.
- **Accidental death insurance**: Regular life insurance already covers accidental death plus everything else.
- **Extended warranties**: Most electronics fail within the manufacturer warranty or last well beyond the extended period. Self-insure with savings.
- **Identity theft insurance**: Most of the protection these policies offer is available for free through your bank or credit card.

## Reviewing Your Coverage

Review all insurance policies annually and whenever a major life event occurs (marriage, home purchase, birth of a child, job change, significant asset growth). Ensure:

- Coverage amounts still match your needs.
- You are not paying for coverage you no longer need.
- You are getting competitive rates — quotes from multiple insurers.
- Beneficiaries are up to date on life insurance and retirement accounts.

## Key Takeaway

Insurance is the foundation of a financial plan — it protects everything else you build. Focus on catastrophic coverage, skip the small stuff, keep deductibles high, and review annually. The best insurance is the kind you never need to use, but would be devastated without.
